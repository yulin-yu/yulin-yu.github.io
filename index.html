About

I am a 1st year PhD student in School of Information at University of Michigan, where I am advised by Paramveer Dhillon and Kentaro Toyama. My research interest is broadly in computational social science and digital humanity. Currently, I actively collaborate research with social and computer scientists.

I got M.S. in information science at University of Michigan under the supervision of Kentaro Toyama, Daniel Romero, and Ceren Budak on data-centric analysis of social justice issues. Before that, I receiced B.A. in Journalism & Art History at Indiana University, where I worked with Yong-Yeol Ahn on computational lyrics analysis. During my undergrad, I worked as a multimedia journalist and report stories on social justice( HIV in Uganda) and cultural diversity , at the Daily Monitor (leading media at Uganda), Indiana Daily Student, and Inside Magazine for years.

Feel free to reach out and chat!
